Scientific Publications

JARVIS: Adaptive Dual-Hemisphere Architectures For Personalized Large Agentic Models

Conference: Adjunct Proceedings of the 33rd ACM Conference on User Modeling, Adaptation and Personalization (UMAP Adjunct '25)

This paper proposes JARVIS, a novel approach to provide Large Language Models (LLMs) with enhanced personalization through a two-hemisphere architecture inspired by the biological organization of the human brain. The system follows a Large Agentic Model (LAM) architecture where the subjective hemisphere dynamically models user preferences and iteratively optimizes behaviors through a training phase grounded on LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation), DPO (Direct Preference Optimization), human feedback, and synthetic data generation ("digital dreams").
